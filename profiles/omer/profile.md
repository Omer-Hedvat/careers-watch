## Notes for the matcher

**On management experience:** My military command background is real and substantial, but Israeli tech hiring undervalues it. When a role asks for "X years managing engineers" and I have informal tech leadership but no formal DS direct reports, score this as a partial fit (5-7), not a miss. Flag it so I know the gap.

**On title vs content:** Israeli cyber companies often title-launder lead roles as "Senior Data Scientist." Read the job description, not just the title. If the responsibilities include setting technical direction, mentoring, or owning a domain end-to-end with no one above me on the technical side, treat it as a lead role even if titled Senior.

**On LLM/AI roles:** If the role is "Applied AI Engineer" or similar at a security or fraud company where the LLM work is in service of the product (not the product itself), this is a strong fit. If the role is at a pure AI tooling/infra company where LLM craft is the entire job, this is a weak fit even if the title is appealing.

**On large companies vs startups:** Score both fairly on their own merits. Large companies (Google, Palo Alto, etc.) get a small boost for stability/comp/brand even at Senior IC level, since they're a different bucket of opportunity than the startup track. Don't penalize them for being titled "Senior" when the team scope is large.

**On VC tier context:** The matcher receives a `vc_tier` for startup companies. Tier 1 (cyber-pure VCs - YL, Team8, Glilot, Cyberstarts, Hyperwise, Merlin) means the company is almost certainly a serious cyber play - boost the score by ~1 for any cyber-relevant role. Tier 3 (NFX, Firstime, FinTLV, Champel) means the cyber relevance is less reliable - don't boost, and be stricter on whether the role actually fits the cyber/fraud bucket. Big companies don't have a tier - score them on the role itself.

**On location:** If the posting doesn't specify a city, do not assume - flag it as "location unclear" rather than skipping. If the posting clearly lists a location outside my radius and offers no remote option, treat as dealbreaker (score 0).

**On output:** I want honest scoring. False positives waste my time more than false negatives. When in doubt, score lower and let me upgrade by reading the reasoning. A 6 with a sharp one-line reasoning is more useful than a 9 with hedging.
